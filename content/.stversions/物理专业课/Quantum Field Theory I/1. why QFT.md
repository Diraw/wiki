## principle of locality

**Newtonian Mechanic**: action of a **distance**

**Faraday (1830)**: All points in space participate in the physical process; effects propagate **from point to neighboring points**

**Field**: a mathmatical device (vehicle) that the principle of locality is at work
- **associated with each point in the space**
- **a dynamical variable**

- **electric field**: $\vec{E}_{\vec{x}}(t)\equiv\vec{E}(\vec{x},t)$ ($\vec{x}$ is just a lebel)
- **magnetc field**: $\vec{B}_{\vec{x}}(t)$ 
- **Maxwell eqn**:
	- containts $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{B}$ depends at same position and time (is cocal)

**whether local**: 
- **at same pt**
- **finite # of deriative**

**ex2: Einstein's gravaty**

**tensor**: $g_{\mu \nu}(\vec{x},t)$ (**metric**)

## From CFT to QFT

1. **classical mechanics**: 
	- **action**: $\begin{aligned}S=\int \mathrm{d}tL(x,\dot{x},t)\end{aligned}$
	- **canonical momentum**: $p=\dfrac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}}$
	- **Hamiltonian**: $H=p\dot{x}-L$
	- **Equation of motion (EOM)**: extremize $S[x(t)]$
		- **variation**: $\delta S=0$ $\Rightarrow$ **trajectory**
2. **classical field theory**: 
	- $\begin{aligned}L=\int \mathrm{d}^{3}x\mathcal{L}(\phi_{a},\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})\end{aligned}$ (local)
		- is significantly constrained
		- $\mathcal{L}$ : lagrange density
		- $\phi_{a}$ is a general field
	- **remarks**: 
		- **not allow** $\begin{aligned}L\sim\int \mathrm{d}^{3}x\mathrm{d}^{3}y(\vec{x},t)\phi(\vec{y},t)\end{aligned}$ ($\vec{x}$ and $\vec{y}$ is different pt)
		- **only allow** one derivative $\partial_\mu \phi$ $\Rightarrow$ EOM contains two time deriative
	- **canonical momentum desnsity**: $\Pi_{a}(\vec{x},t)=\dfrac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \dot{\phi}_{a}(\vec{x},t)}$ or $\dfrac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial(\partial_{t}\phi_{a})}$ 
	- **Hamitonian density**: $\mathcal{H}(\vec{x},t)=\Pi_{a}\dot{\phi}_{a}-\mathcal{L}$ 
		- **Hamitonian**: $\begin{aligned}H=\int \mathrm{d}^{3}x\mathcal{H}(\vec{x},t)\end{aligned}$ 
	- **Equation of motion**: $\delta S=0$ 
		- **action**: $\begin{aligned}S=\int \mathrm{d}tL=\int \mathrm{d}t\mathrm{d}^{3}x\mathcal{L}(\phi_{a},\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})=\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\mathcal{L}(\phi_{a},\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})\end{aligned}$ 
		- $\begin{aligned}0=\delta S=&\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\left[ \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi_{a}} \delta \phi_{a}+\frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial(\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})}\delta(\partial_{\mu }\phi_{a})\right] \\=&\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\left[ \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi_{a}} \delta \phi_{a}+\frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial(\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})}\delta(\partial_{\mu }\phi_{a})\right] \\=&\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\left[ \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi_{a}} \delta \phi_{a}-\partial_{\mu}\frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial(\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})}\delta\phi_{a}\right]+\cancel{\mathrm{boundaryterm}}\\\Rightarrow 0=& \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \phi_{a}}-\partial_{\mu}\frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial(\partial_{\mu}\phi_{a})}\end{aligned}$
	- **ex1: maxwell equation**
		- dynamic variable: $A_{\mu}(x)$
		- field of **Strength** $F_{\mu \nu}=\partial_{\mu}A_{\nu}-\partial_{\nu}A_{\mu}$ 
		- $\begin{aligned}S=\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\left( \frac{1}{4}F_{\mu \nu} F^{\mu \nu}\right)\end{aligned}$ 
	- **ex2: Einstein-Hilbert action** 
		- $\begin{aligned}S=\frac{1}{16\pi G_{N}}\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\sqrt{ -g }R\end{aligned}$ 
		- $g$ : metric
		- $R$ : Ricci scalar
	- **ex3: scalar field theory** 
		- real value: $\phi(x)$ 
		- $\begin{aligned}S=\int \mathrm{d}^{4}x\left[ -\frac{1}{2}\partial_{\mu}\phi\partial^{\mu}\phi-v(\phi) \right]\end{aligned}$ 
		- translation symmetry $\Rightarrow$ all parameters in $v(x)$ must be constant
		- $\Pi(x)=\dfrac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \dot{\phi}}=\dot{\phi}(x)$ ($\partial_{\mu}\phi\partial^{\mu}\phi=-\dot{\phi}^{2}+(\nabla \phi)^{2}$)
		- $\mathcal{H}=\Pi\dot{\phi}-\mathcal{L}=\dfrac{\pi^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{1}{2}(\nabla \phi)^{2}+v(\phi)$
		- EOM: $\partial^{2}\phi-\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial \phi}=0$ ($\partial^{2}=\partial_{\mu}\partial^{\mu}=-\partial t^{2}+\nabla^{2}$) 
			- simplest case: $v(\phi)=f\phi$ ($f$ is a constant from principle of locality) 
				- $\Rightarrow$ EOM: $\partial^{2}\phi=f$ ($f$ is an external force) 
			- $v(\phi)=\dfrac{1}{2}m^{2}\phi^{2}$ ($m$ : mass of particle)
				- $\Rightarrow$ EOM: $\partial^{2}\phi-m^{2}\phi=0$ (Klein-Gorden eqn) 

# symmetry and conversation

- **invariant**

		- **rotationally invariant** (3)

- **symmetry**:
	1. **transformation**: $\phi_{a}\rightarrow \phi'_{a}$ which leaves the **action invariant** 
		- 4 continuous parameters
		- ex: $x^{\mu}\rightarrow x^{\mu'}+a^\mu$ where $a^{\mu}$ is a const vector $\Rightarrow$ $\phi(x)=\phi'(x')$ 
			- a scalar field transfroms under a general spacetime transformation
	2. **lorentz transformation**: $x^{\mu}\rightarrow x^{\mu'}=\Lambda^{\mu}_{\nu}x^{\nu}$ 
		- 6 continuous parameters
		- $\Lambda^{\mu}_{\nu}$ : cosntant lorentz transformation 

continuous symmetry: Both `1` and `2` are continuous symmetries
discrete symmetry: $Z_{2}$ : parity、reflection

global symmetries: transformation parameters are spacetime independent
local symmetries: ……dependent

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
