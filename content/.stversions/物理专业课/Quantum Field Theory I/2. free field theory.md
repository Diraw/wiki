# Quantization of Harmonic Oscillator in the Heisenberg Picture

$\ddot{x}+\omega^{2}x=0$
classical solution: $x(t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2 }}(ae^{-i\omega t}+a^{*}e^{-i\omega t})$ 

$\partial_{t}^{2}\hat{x}+\omega^{2}\hat{x}=0$
quantum solution: $\hat{x}(t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2 }}(\hat{a}e^{-i\omega t}+\hat{a}^{\dagger}e^{i\omega t})$ 
canonical momentum: $\hat{p}=\partial_{t}\hat{x}$ 
canonical quantization condition: $[\hat{x}(t),\hat{p}(t)]=i$ $\Rightarrow$ $[\hat{a},\hat{a}^{\dagger}]=1$ 
Hilbert space: $\hat{a}\ket{0}=0$ , $\ket{\hat{n}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{ n! }}(\hat{a}^{\dagger})^{n}\ket{0}$ 

summary: **Steps of Quantization** 
1. classical eom -> quantum operator eqn
2. find most general solution to classical eom 
3. promote integration consts ($a,a^{*}$) to constant quantum operators $\Rightarrow$ full time evolution at quantum level
4. impose canonical quantization conditions
5. constant operators, which can be used to generalize Hilbert space

For free scalar field theory:

Fourier transformation: $\phi(x)=e^{-iEt+i\vec{k}\cdot \vec{x}}$ (plane wave)
$E^{2}=\vec{k}^{2}+m^{2}\equiv\omega_{k}^{2}$ $\Rightarrow$ $u_{\vec{k}}=e^{-i\omega_{\vec{k}}t+i\vec{k}\cdot \vec{x}}$ (positive energy soln) and $u_{\vec{k}}^{*}=e^{i\omega_{\vec{k}}t-i\vec{k}\cdot \vec{x}}$ (negative energy soln) 
$\phi(x)=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\frac{1}{\sqrt{ 2\omega_{\vec{k}} }}(a_{\vec{k}}u_{\vec{k}}+a^{*}_{\vec{k}}u^{*}_{\vec{k}})$ $\Rightarrow$ $\hat{\phi}$ with $\hat{a}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}^{*}_{\vec{k}}$ , $\hat{\Pi}=\partial_{t}\hat{\phi}$
canonical commutation relation: $[\hat{\phi}(x),\hat{\Pi}(x)]=?$ 
Hints: for multiple particle: 
- $[\hat{x}_{a}(t),\hat{p}_{b}(t)]=i\delta_{ab}$ (kronecker delta)
- $[\hat{x}_{a},\hat{x}_{b}]=0$ , $[\hat{p}_{a},\hat{p}_{b}]=0$ 
- $\Rightarrow$ 
	- $[\hat{\phi}(t,\vec{x}),\hat{\phi}(t,\vec{x}')]=0$ 
	- $[\hat{\Pi}(t,\vec{x}),\hat{\Pi}(t,\vec{x}')]=0$
	- $[\hat{\phi}(t,\vec{x}),\hat{\pi}(t,\vec{x}')]=i\delta^{(3)}(\vec{x}-\vec{x}')$ (dirac delta) 
	- $\Rightarrow$ 
		- $[\hat{a}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}_{\vec{k}'}]=[\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}'}]=0$ 
		- $[\hat{a}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}'}]=(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}_{(k)}(\vec{k}-\vec{k}')$ 

$\hat{H}=\int \mathrm{d}^{3}x\mathcal{H}=\cdots=\frac{1}{2}\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}(\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}^{\dagger}\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}+\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}^{\dagger})=\frac{1}{2}\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}\left( \hat{a}_{\vec{k}}^{\dagger}\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}+\frac{1}{2}[\hat{a}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}^{\dagger}] \right)$ 
zero-point energy: $E_{0}=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}\frac{1}{2}[\hat{a}_{\vec{k}},\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}^{\dagger}]=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}\frac{1}{2}(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(0)$ , which is divergent

**Remarks**:

2. Lorentz symmetry: $M^{\mu \nu}$ 

$E_{0}=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}\frac{1}{2}(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(0)E_{0}=\frac{1}{2}(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(0)\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\omega_{\vec{k}}$

**Hilbert space**

ground state(vaccum): $\hat{a}_{\vec{k}}\ket{0}=\ket{0}$ 
general stete: $\ket{\hat{a}_{\vec{k_{1}}}^{\dagger},\hat{a}_{\vec{k_{2}}}^{\dagger},\cdots}=\left(\hat{a}_{\vec{k_{1}}}^{\dagger}\right)^{n_{\vec{k}_{1}}}\left(\hat{a}_{\vec{k_{2}}}^{\dagger}\right)^{n_{\vec{k}_{2}}}\cdots\ket{0}$
- $\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}}\ket{0}=\ket{\vec{k}}$ 
- $\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}_{1}}\hat{a}^{\dagger}_{\vec{k}_{2}}\ket{0}=\ket{\vec{k}_{1},\vec{k}_{2}}$ 

ground state:
- $\hat{H}\ket{0}=0$ 
- $P^{i}\ket{0}=0$ 
general state:
- $\hat{H}\ket{\vec{k}}=w_{\vec{k}}\ket{\vec{k}}$ 
- $\hat{P}^{i}\ket{\vec{k}}=k_{i}\ket{\vec{k}}$ 
- $\ket{\vec{k}}$ has $P^{\mu}=(w_{\vec{k}},\vec{k})$ : momentum of a relativistic particle of mass $m$ 

$\ket{\vec{k}_{1},\vec{k}_{2}}$ : $E=\omega_{\vec{k}_{1}}+\omega_{\vec{k}_{2}}$ , $\vec{k}=\vec{k}_{1}+\vec{k}_{2}$ $\Rightarrow$ $(E,\vec{k})$ : two particles of momentum $(\omega_{\vec{k}_{1}},\vec{k}_{1})$ and $(\omega_{\vec{k}_{2}},\vec{k}_{2})$ 

$\ket{n_{\vec{k}_{1}},n_{\vec{k}_{2}}}$ : $n_{\vec{k}_{1}}$ particles of momentum $\vec{k}_{1}$, $n_{\vec{k}_{2}}$ particles of momentum $\vec{k}_{2}$ 

**Remarks**:
1. this can describe any # of particles
2. $\left[a_{\vec{k}_{1}}^{\dagger},a_{\vec{k}_{2}}^{\dagger}\right]=0$ full symmetry in permuting particles in a general state $\Rightarrow$ bosons
3. all particles are  positive energies: even $E^{2}=\vec{k}^{2}+m^{2}$ has two solutions, when we look at the eigenstate, we only have positive energy
4. total energy of state = sum of energies of all particles (only for no interaction, a theory of free particle)
5. normalization: 
	- $\bra{\vec{k}}\ket{\vec{k}}=(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(\vec{k}-\vec{k}')$ is not nice
	- $\ket{k}\equiv \sqrt{ 2\omega_{\vec{k}} }\ket{\vec{k}}=\sqrt{ 2\omega_{\vec{k}} }\hat{a}_{k}^{\dagger}\ket{0}$ 
	- $\bra{k}\ket{k'}=2\omega_{\vec{k}}(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(\vec{k}-\vec{k}')$ 
		- $\omega_{\vec{k}}(2\pi)^{3}\delta^{(3)}(\vec{k}-\vec{k}')$ transforms nicely under Lorentz transformation
		- can show $u_{\Lambda}\ket{k}=\ket{\Lambda k}$ 
			- $\Lambda$ : Lorentz transformation
			- $u_{\Lambda}$ : quantum operator
6. general single-particle states $\ket{\psi}=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}\vec{k}}{(2\pi)^{3}}f(\vec{k})\ket{k}$ 
	- can shoose $f(\vec{k})$ to construct a localized (in space) wave packet
		- $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}\vec{k}_{1}}{(2\pi)^{3}}\frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}\vec{k}_{2}}{(2\pi)^{3}}f(\vec{k}_{1})f(\vec{k}_{2})\ket{k_{1},k_{2}}$ 
7. structure of Hilbert spcae $\mathcal{H}=\ket{0}\oplus \mathcal{H}_{1}\oplus \mathcal{H}_{2}\cdots$ 
	- the set of states with a finite # of particle is called a Fock space
8. $\ket{\vec{k}},\ket{k},\ket{k_{1},k_{2}}$ are not normalizable, just as $\psi=e^{ikx}$ in NR QM
	- not general state
	- $\bra{\psi}\ket{\psi}=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}^{3}k}{(2\pi)^{3}}\left|f(k)\right|^{2}<\infty$ 
	- then $\ket{\psi}$ is normalizable state (with choosing a basis $\{f_{i}\}$ )
